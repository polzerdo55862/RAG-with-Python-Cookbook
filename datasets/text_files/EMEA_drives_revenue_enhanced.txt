Europe, Middle East, and Africa Drives Revenue Surge in Second Quarter of 2019: Business Development Team Delivers Strong Year-over-Year Growth

In a significant performance during the second quarter of 2019, the Business Development team has driven a substantial increase in sales volume, with Europe, Middle East, and Africa emerging as the key growth engine. The region demonstrated remarkable sales activity, contributing to a Year-over-Year increase of up to 2.3 times.

Key Highlights:
* Europe, Middle East, and Africa Dominance: Sales activity across 20 countries in Europe, Middle East, and Africa fueled the impressive performance in the second quarter of 2019.
* Top Contributors: The United States (28%), Spain (20%), India (15%), and Norway (11%) were the primary contributors to the overall sales volume in the second quarter of 2019.
* Year-over-Year Growth: The Business Development team achieved a significant Year-over-Year increase in sales volume, demonstrating strong execution and ability to enter and expand in new markets.

Breakdown by Region:
* Europe, Middle East, and Africa: The standout performer, this region delivered a substantial portion of the overall sales volume, driven by robust sales activity in key markets.
* Americas: While contributing a notable share, the Americas region showed a more moderate growth trajectory compared to Europe, Middle East, and Africa.
* Asia-Pacific: The Asia-Pacific region experienced a relatively stable quarter in terms of sales volume.

**Brief Explanation of Technical Terms:**

1. **Revenue Surge**: This means that the company made a lot more money than it did before.

2. **Sales Volume**: This refers to the amount of products sold or services provided.

3. **Growth Engine**: This is like the part that makes everything run and grow, similar to a motor in a car but for the business.

4. **Market Penetration**: This means how well the company is doing in selling its products in a new area or market.

5. **Moderate Growth Trajectory**: This means the growth path is not very fast or very slow—it's in the middle.
